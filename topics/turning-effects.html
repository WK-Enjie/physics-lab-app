⚖️ Turning Effects of Forces

Moments, principle of moments, conditions for equilibrium, centre of gravity & classic problems

🔧 Moment of a Force

Moment (Torque) Moment = Force × Perpendicular distance from pivot
In symbols τ = F × d    (unit: N m)

Understanding Moment

A moment (or torque) is the turning effect of a force about a pivot. It depends on:

  • The magnitude of the force — a larger force produces a larger moment.
  • The perpendicular distance from the line of action of the force to the pivot.
💡 "Perpendicular distance" means the shortest distance from the pivot to the line of action of the force — not the length of the lever!

Direction of Moments

  • Clockwise (CW) — the force tends to rotate the object clockwise.
  • Anticlockwise (ACW) — the force tends to rotate anticlockwise.

🔧 Opening a Door

The handle is far from the hinge to maximise perpendicular distance, producing a larger moment with less force.

🔩 Long Spanner

A longer spanner means larger d, so a smaller F is needed for the same moment.

⚖️ Principle of Moments

Principle of Moments For rotational equilibrium:
Sum of CW moments = Sum of ACW moments
💡 Smart Pivot Choice: Take moments about a point where an unknown force acts — that force has zero moment (d = 0), simplifying calculations!

Conditions for Complete Equilibrium

  1. Translational: Resultant force = 0
  2. Rotational: Resultant moment about any point = 0
📝 Worked Example 1 — Simple Seesaw

Q: A uniform 4 m beam (200 N) is pivoted at centre. 300 N is placed 1.5 m left of pivot. Where must 500 N be placed on the right to balance?

1

Beam weight acts at pivot → moment = 0.

2

ACW = 300 × 1.5 = 450 N m

3

CW = 500 × d = 450 → d = 0.90 m

📝 Worked Example 2 — Non-Central Pivot

Q: Uniform plank: 10 kg, 4 m long. Pivot at 1.5 m from left. 5 kg hangs from left end. Find force F at right end to balance. (g = 10 m/s²)

1

Plank weight = 100 N at centre (2 m from left). Mass weight = 50 N at left end.

2

ACW: 50 × 1.5 = 75 N m

3

CW: 100 × 0.5 + F × 2.5

4

75 = 50 + 2.5F → F = 10 N

🎯 Centre of Gravity (CG)

Definition The point where the entire weight of the object may be considered to act.
  • For uniform objects, CG is at the geometric centre.
  • For irregular shapes, use the plumb line method.
  • CG need not be inside the object (e.g. a ring).

Stability

🔺

Stable

CG rises when displaced → returns to original position. Wide base, low CG.

🔻

Unstable

CG falls when displaced → topples. Narrow base, high CG.

⚪

Neutral

CG stays at same height → stays in new position.

💡 Toppling: An object topples when the vertical line from CG falls outside the base. To improve stability: lower CG and/or widen base.

🔄 Couple

Couple Two equal, opposite, non-coincident forces → pure rotation
Torque = F × d (d = distance between forces)

🚗 Steering Wheel

Both hands apply equal opposite forces → pure rotation, no translation.
